## Target Audience

### Primary — Sellers
**Who:** Small and medium traders in Nigerian physical markets
**Where:** Alaba International Market, Computer Village (Ikeja), Balogun Market, Trade Fair, Bodija (Ibadan), Ariaria (Aba), Wuse Market (Abuja)
**Pain points:**
- Buyers can't find them beyond their physical stall
- WhatsApp commerce is unorganised and hard to scale
- No professional storefront without expensive development
- Writing product listings is time-consuming

**What MarketX gives them:**
- A storefront set up in minutes
- A map pin that makes them discoverable to nearby buyers
- AI that writes their listing from a phone photo
- An inbox that consolidates all buyer messages

---

### Secondary — Buyers
**Who:** Urban Nigerians aged 18–40 who shop locally but want the convenience of digital
**Where:** Lagos primarily, expanding to Abuja, PH, Kano
**Pain points:**
- Don't know which traders are near them
- Have to ask around or call multiple people to find a specific product
- WhatsApp commerce has no transaction safety
- Hard to compare prices or verify seller legitimacy

**What MarketX gives them:**
- A map that shows who is selling near them right now
- Store previews with products, hours, and distance before committing
- A verified badge system that signals trust
- Dassa — an AI assistant that finds products and manages their cart through conversation

## Market Squares Strategy

Market Squares are not just a feature — they are the core competitive advantage.

**What they are:** Digital zones on the map that mirror real physical markets (Alaba, Computer Village, etc.)

**Why they matter:**
- Buyers already trust these market names — "Computer Village" means something in Lagos
- Grouping sellers under a known market name reduces discovery friction
- Squares create a natural entry point for bulk onboarding (sign up 20 Alaba sellers at once)

**Onboarding approach (MVP):**
- Identify 2–3 key markets in Lagos for first cohort
- Personally onboard 10–15 sellers per market
- Use Square as a marketing hook: "Your store on the Computer Village map"

## Content Strategy

### Platforms
| Platform | Purpose | Cadence |
|---|---|---|
| Instagram | Product demos, seller stories, feature highlights | 4x per week |
| TikTok | Short-form demos, Dassa interactions, market culture | 3x per week |
| Facebook | Seller onboarding announcements, community posts | 2x per week |
| WhatsApp Broadcast | Direct seller outreach and onboarding | As needed |

### Content Pillars
1. **Platform demos** — Show features in action. Map discovery, AI listing tool, Dassa chat.
2. **Seller stories** — Real traders, real storefronts. Show the before/after.
3. **Market Square spotlights** — Highlight a specific market and its sellers.
4. **Buyer-side tips** — How to use the map, how to talk to Dassa, how to find deals.
5. **Behind the build** — Occasional behind-the-scenes. Builds trust and personality.

### MVP Phase Content Priorities
1. Seller onboarding walkthrough video (how to set up a storefront in under 5 minutes)
2. AI listing generator demo (phone photo → full listing in seconds)
3. Map discovery demo (GPS → nearby stores → store preview → visit)
4. Dassa demo (buyer asks a question → Dassa finds products and adds to cart)
