# Vision & Mission

---

## Vision

**Every Nigerian market trader has a digital storefront that works as hard as they do.**

The trader at Computer Village who fixes phones. The fabric seller at Balogun. The fashion vendor at Trade Fair. Every one of them deserves to be discoverable, shoppable, and connected to buyers — not just the ones who happen to walk past their stall.

That is the world MarketX is building toward.

---

## Mission

**To connect Nigerian buyers with nearby sellers through technology that is simple enough for any trader to use and powerful enough to replace WhatsApp commerce.**

We do this by building:
- A map that shows you who is selling near you, right now
- Storefronts that any trader can set up in minutes — with AI handling the hard parts
- An AI assistant that helps buyers shop like they have a personal market guide
- Digital Market Squares that mirror the physical ones Nigerians already know and trust

---

## What We Believe

**Local commerce is already working — it just needs better infrastructure.**
Nigerian market traders move billions of naira every day through physical markets, phone calls, and WhatsApp. We are not replacing that behaviour. We are giving it a platform that makes it faster, more trusted, and more discoverable.

**AI should reduce the gap between informal and formal commerce.**
A trader who cannot write product descriptions in formal English should not be at a disadvantage. AI listing tools, Dassa, and smart search exist to level that playing field — not to add complexity.

**Market Squares are a competitive moat, not just a feature.**
Bodija, Alaba International, Computer Village, Ariaria — these are cultural landmarks with existing buyer trust. Digitising them as named, browsable zones on a map gives MarketX something Google Maps and Jumia cannot easily replicate.

**Validated behaviour beats planned features.**
Every sprint, we are asking: did a real trader use this without help? Did a real buyer complete a purchase? If not, we fix that before building anything new.

---

## Strategic Priorities (MVP Phase)

1. Seller activation — 30 traders with live, maintained storefronts
2. Buyer conversion — 60% of registered buyers complete a discovery-to-transaction flow
3. AI adoption — 50% of sellers use the listing generator at least once
4. Platform trust — zero critical incidents in the first 30 days post-launch

---

## Where We Will Not Go (Yet)

- Multi-country before Nigerian cities are proven
- Logistics before the demand signal is confirmed
- Enterprise or B2B before the individual trader is well served
- Native mobile app before the web experience is validated
